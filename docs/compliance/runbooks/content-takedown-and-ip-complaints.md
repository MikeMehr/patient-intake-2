# Content Takedown and Intellectual Property Complaints Procedure

## Purpose

Define the procedure for receiving, evaluating, and responding to allegations that
content published on or through Health Assist AI is:

- Libellous or defamatory
- Infringing a third party's intellectual property rights (copyright, trademark, patent)
- In violation of a third party's privacy rights
- Otherwise unlawful under applicable law

This procedure satisfies insurance requirement items (n) and (o) of the Cyber Extension
Statement of Fact and supports compliance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act
(DMCA, 17 U.S.C. § 512) for US-connected users, as well as analogous Canadian
provisions under the Copyright Act (R.S.C. 1985, c. C-42).

## Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Content published on the public-facing Health Assist AI website and marketing pages
- Content accessible via the Health Assist AI platform to authenticated users
- AI-generated outputs produced by the platform (insofar as Health Assist AI controls them)
- Content submitted by healthcare organizations or providers via the platform

**Not in scope:**
Health Assist AI does not operate public message boards, open forums, or social media
channels. Patient data is not publicly displayed. The primary content takedown risk
relates to platform documentation, marketing content, and AI-generated clinical
document templates.

## Types of Complaints

### 1. Copyright Infringement (DMCA / Canadian Copyright Act)

A copyright infringement notice must include:

1. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed
2. Identification of the material on Health Assist AI that is claimed to be infringing,
   with sufficient detail to locate it (URL, description, screenshot)
3. Your contact information (name, address, telephone, email)
4. A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use is not authorized by the
   copyright owner, its agent, or the law
5. A statement that the information in the notice is accurate, and under penalty of
   perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner
6. Your physical or electronic signature

Send to: **info@health-assist.org** with subject line: `COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT NOTICE`

### 2. Trademark Infringement

A trademark complaint must identify:

1. The trademark(s) allegedly infringed (with registration number if applicable)
2. The specific use on the Health Assist AI platform that is alleged to infringe
3. Your contact information and authority to act on behalf of the trademark owner
4. A good faith statement that the use is unauthorized

Send to: **info@health-assist.org** with subject line: `TRADEMARK COMPLAINT`

### 3. Defamation / Libel Allegations

A defamation complaint must identify:

1. The specific content alleged to be defamatory and where it appears
2. Why the content is alleged to be false and harmful to the complainant
3. Your contact information

Send to: **info@health-assist.org** with subject line: `DEFAMATION COMPLAINT`

### 4. Privacy Rights Violations

A privacy complaint must identify:

1. The specific content or data alleged to violate privacy rights
2. The applicable law or right alleged to be violated (e.g., PIPEDA, BC PIPA, GDPR)
3. Your contact information

Send to: **info@health-assist.org** with subject line: `PRIVACY COMPLAINT`

## Response Procedure

### Step 1 — Acknowledgment (within 5 business days)

Upon receipt of a complaint:

- Acknowledge receipt to the complainant
- Assign a case reference number
- Log the complaint in the incident/complaint register with: date received, complainant,
  type of complaint, content at issue, assigned reviewer

### Step 2 — Evaluation (within 10 business days of acknowledgment)

The Security/Privacy Officer evaluates:

- Is the complaint complete and credible?
- Is the content actually present on Health Assist AI systems?
- Is the content controlled by Health Assist AI, or by a customer/third-party?
- What is the legal risk exposure?
- Does the complaint require external legal review?

### Step 3 — Action

Based on evaluation, one of the following actions is taken:

| Outcome | Action |
|---|---|
| Complaint is valid and content is controlled by Health Assist AI | Remove or modify content within 15 business days of original complaint |
| Content is customer-controlled PHI or healthcare data | Notify the relevant healthcare organization; PHI is governed by their responsibilities |
| Complaint is incomplete | Request additional information from complainant |
| Complaint is not credible or lacks legal basis | Issue written response with reasoning; no takedown |
| Complaint requires legal review | Engage legal counsel before responding |

### Step 4 — Counter-Notification (DMCA)

If content is taken down in response to a DMCA notice and the content provider believes
the takedown was in error, they may submit a counter-notification including:

1. Identification of the removed material and its prior location
2. A statement under penalty of perjury that the material was removed by mistake or
   misidentification
3. The submitter's contact information and consent to jurisdiction
4. The submitter's physical or electronic signature

Counter-notifications are sent to: **info@health-assist.org**

Upon receipt of a valid counter-notification, Health Assist AI will:
- Forward the counter-notification to the original complainant
- Restore the material within 10–14 business days unless the complainant files a court action

### Step 5 — Record Keeping

All complaints and responses are retained for a minimum of 3 years, including:
- Original complaint
- Evaluation notes
- Action taken and date
- Any counter-notification

## Repeat Infringer Policy

Health Assist AI will, in appropriate circumstances, terminate the accounts of users
(including healthcare organizations) who are found to be repeat infringers of third-party
intellectual property rights.
